The conservation of angular momentum helps explain many observed phenomena, for example the increase in rotational speed of a spinning figure skater (picture_2) as the skater's arms are contracted, the high rotational rates of neutron stars, the Coriolis effect, and the precession of gyroscopes. In general, conservation does limit the possible motion of a system, but does not uniquely determine what the exact motion is.
